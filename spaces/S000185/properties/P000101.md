Let $f:X\to X$ be defined by $f((m,n))=(\max(m,n),\max(m,n))$,
$f(\infty_x)=\infty_y$, and $f(\infty_y)=\infty_y$.

Then $f$ is continuous and witnesses a retract $\{(p,p):p<\omega\}\cup\{\infty_y\}$
which is missing its limit point $\infty_x$.
