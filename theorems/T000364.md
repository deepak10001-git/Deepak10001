Mentioned in the proof of Proposition 5 in {{doi:10.1016/j.topol.2005.07.015}}.

Given any open cover $\mathscr U$ of a $k$-Lindel√∂f space $X$, consider $\mathscr V = \left\{ \bigcup \mathscr F : \mathscr F \in [\mathscr U]^{<\omega} \right\}$ where $[\mathscr U]^{<\omega}$ denotes the set of finite subsets of $\mathscr U$. Then $\mathscr V$ is a $k$-cover of $X$ and we can find a countable subset which is a $k$-cover of $X$. Notice that such a subset corresponds to a countable subcover of $\mathscr U$.
