There exists a point $p \in X$ and a continuous map $F : X \times [0, 1] \to X$ such that for all $x \in X$, $F(x, 0) = x$ and $F(x, 1) = p$.

Defined on page 330 in exercise 3 of section 51 in {{zb:0951.54001}}.
